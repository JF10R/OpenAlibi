How to play

OpenAlibi rules

  1. Place every character. The victim has a name beginning with V and must always be placed last. Every testimony is true.
  2. Respect rows and columns. Two people can never share a row or column.
  3. Respect the map. Blocking furniture cannot be occupied. Chairs, beds, rugs and puddles can.
  4. “Next to” is orthogonal. The cell must be directly above, below, left or right, and in the same room.
  5. The murderer is inferred automatically. When answers are checked, the person alone with the victim is identified as the murderer.

Rectangular grids use at most min(rows, columns) characters to preserve the row-column constraint.
